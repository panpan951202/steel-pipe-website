Pipeline corrosion remains one of the most significant challenges facing the oil & gas and water industries, with corrosion-related failures costing billions annually. Three-layer polyethylene (3PE) and polypropylene (3PP) coating systems have emerged as the gold standard for external corrosion protection.

## What is 3PE/3PP Coating?

A three-layer coating system consists of:

1. **Layer 1 — Fusion Bonded Epoxy (FBE) Primer**: Provides excellent adhesion to the steel surface and acts as the primary corrosion barrier (thickness: 60-150μm)
2. **Layer 2 — Copolymer Adhesive**: Chemically bonds the epoxy primer to the polyolefin top layer (thickness: 150-350μm)
3. **Layer 3 — Polyethylene (PE) or Polypropylene (PP)**: Provides mechanical protection against impacts, soil stress, and moisture ingress (thickness: 1.5-4.0mm)

## Key Benefits

- **Superior Adhesion**: The FBE layer chemically bonds to the prepared steel surface
- **Excellent Cathodic Disbondment Resistance**: Minimal coating disbondment under cathodic protection
- **Temperature Range**: 3PE suitable to 80°C, 3PP suitable to 110°C
- **Impact Resistance**: Outstanding mechanical protection during handling, transport, and installation
- **Long Service Life**: Designed for 50+ years in typical buried or subsea environments

## Application Process

The 3PE/3PP application process is highly automated and controlled:

1. Surface preparation (shot/sand blasting to Sa 2.5 near-white standard)
2. Pre-heating of the pipe
3. Electrostatic application of FBE powder
4. Application of copolymer adhesive (extruded or powder)
5. Application of PE/PP top layer (side extrusion or spiral wrapping)
6. Water quenching for cooling
7. Holiday detection and thickness measurement

## Industry Standards

3PE/3PP coating systems are governed by international standards including DIN 30670 / DIN 30678, CSA Z245.21, ISO 21809-1, NFA 49-710 / 49-711, and GB/T 23257.
