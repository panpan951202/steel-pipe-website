Material selection is arguably the most critical decision in pipeline engineering. The right choice ensures safe, reliable operation over decades; the wrong choice can lead to catastrophic failure. This guide provides a practical framework for selecting steel pipes for oil and gas transportation applications.

## Design Considerations

Before selecting materials, engineers must evaluate design pressure and temperature, fluid composition (including H₂S content for sour service), environmental conditions (buried, above-ground, subsea), geotechnical considerations, installation method, and applicable codes.

## Material Grade Selection

### API 5L Grade B to X52
Suitable for low to medium pressure gathering lines and distribution networks. Good weldability and formability, cost-effective for general service.

### API 5L Grade X60 to X70
Suitable for medium to high pressure transmission pipelines. Optimal balance of strength and toughness, most commonly specified for cross-country pipelines.

### API 5L Grade X80 to X100
Suitable for high-pressure, large-diameter transmission. Higher strength allows thinner walls — bringing weight and cost savings — but requires specialized welding procedures.

## Pipe Type Selection Guide

| Application | Recommended Pipe Type |
|------------|----------------------|
| Onshore transmission (>16") | LSAW or SSAW |
| Onshore gathering (<16") | SMLS or ERW |
| Offshore risers | SMLS |
| Subsea flowlines | SMLS or LSAW |
| Water injection | ERW or SSAW |

## Sour Service Requirements

For H₂S-containing environments, materials must meet NACE MR0175/ISO 15156 with controlled hardness (typically HRC 22 max for carbon steels), HIC testing per NACE TM0284, SSC testing per NACE TM0177, and specialized low-sulfur chemistries.
