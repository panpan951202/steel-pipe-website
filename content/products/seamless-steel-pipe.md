  - question: What is the difference between SMLS and ERW pipes?
    answer: SMLS pipes are manufactured without any welding seam from a solid steel billet, offering superior strength and pressure resistance. ERW pipes are formed from steel coils and welded along the longitudinal seam using electric resistance welding, making them more cost-effective for low to medium pressure applications.

Seamless steel pipes (SMLS) are manufactured through a specialized process that creates a hollow tube from a solid billet of steel, with no welding seams. This manufacturing method ensures uniform strength throughout the pipe circumference, making SMLS pipes ideal for high-pressure and critical applications.

Our seamless steel pipes are produced using the latest hot-rolling and cold-drawing technologies, ensuring precise dimensional tolerances and superior surface finish. With a comprehensive range of sizes and material grades including carbon steel, alloy steel, and stainless steel, our SMLS pipes serve the most demanding industries worldwide.
