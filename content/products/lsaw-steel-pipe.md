faq:
  - question: What is the difference between LSAW and SSAW pipes?
    answer: LSAW pipes have a straight longitudinal seam and are typically used for thick-wall, large-diameter applications requiring high strength and low stress concentration. SSAW pipes have a spiral seam, are more economical, and are well-suited for medium-wall applications and faster production.
  - question: What is the JCOE forming process?
    answer: JCOE (J-shape, C-shape, O-shape, Expansion) is a progressive forming process where steel plate is pressed in stages (J → C → O) to form a pipe, then welded, and finally mechanically expanded. This process ensures excellent dimensional accuracy and uniform mechanical properties.
  - question: Are LSAW pipes suitable for offshore applications?
    answer: Yes, LSAW pipes are the preferred choice for offshore and subsea pipelines, especially in deep-water applications. They can be manufactured to meet stringent offshore standards like DNV-OS-F101 with enhanced toughness requirements.

Longitudinal Submerged Arc Welded (LSAW) steel pipes are manufactured by forming and welding thick steel plates into pipe form using the JCOE or UOE forming process and submerged arc welding technology. The welding is done along the longitudinal axis of the pipe, resulting in a strong, high-quality welded joint.

LSAW pipes are the preferred choice for large-diameter, thick-wall applications in long-distance oil and gas transmission, offshore pipelines, water mains, and construction piling. With wall thicknesses reaching up to 80mm and diameters up to 56 inches, our LSAW pipes deliver exceptional performance in the most demanding infrastructure projects.
